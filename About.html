About KillShift

KillShift is a high-octane, cartoon-style third-person shooter where driving and shooting collide. Players boost, flip, and blast their way across neon battlefields — where chaos is the only rule.

The game combines fast-paced vehicle movement with responsive third-person shooting, creating a fluid and energetic gameplay experience. Players are encouraged to use momentum, mobility, and creative movement to gain the upper hand in combat.

Key Features

  • High-speed vehicle-based combat
  • Stylised, cartoon-inspired visual design
  • Emphasis on player mobility and freedom
  • Fast-paced, arcade-style gameplay

Target Audience

KillShift is aimed at players who enjoy fast-paced arcade shooters and action games. Its accessible mechanics make it easy to pick up, while its movement-focused gameplay provides depth for more experienced players.

Project Vision

The goal of KillShift is to explore how vehicle movement and third-person shooting can be combined into a single, cohesive experience. The project focuses on visual identity, usability, and engaging moment-to-moment gameplay.

Development Timeline

Phase 1
Concept & Planning

Initial concept development focusing on combining driving mechanics with third-person shooting and defining the core gameplay pillars.

Phase 2
Visual Design

Creation of the stylised, neon-inspired visual direction and overall aesthetic to support the arcade-style gameplay.

Phase 3
Gameplay Prototyping

Development and testing of movement, combat flow, and player controls to ensure responsive and engaging gameplay.

Phase 4
Refinement & Testing

Iterative refinement of mechanics and usability based on testing and feedback, focusing on balance and player experience.
